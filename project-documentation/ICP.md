# Ideal Customer Profiles (ICP)

**Last Updated:** 2025-11-24

---

## Overview

Mindmaker serves three distinct ICPs:
1. **Individual Leaders** - C-suite, VPs, senior leaders feeling behind on AI
2. **Executive Teams** - Leadership teams with fragmented AI thinking
3. **Investment Firms** - VCs, PE firms, advisors managing portfolio AI risk

---

## ICP #1: Individual Leaders

### Demographics
- **Title:** CEO, COO, CMO, VP Product, VP Strategy, Chief of Staff
- **Company Size:** 50-5,000 employees
- **Industry:** Tech, SaaS, Professional Services, Finance, Healthcare
- **Revenue:** $10M-$500M ARR (typically Series B to pre-IPO or established companies)
- **Age:** 35-55 years old
- **Education:** MBA or equivalent experience

### Psychographics
- **Pressure:** Feels constant pressure to "do something with AI"
- **Confusion:** Can't separate hype from real opportunities
- **Skepticism:** Tired of vendor pitches that all sound the same
- **Confidence Gap:** Worries about making the wrong AI investment
- **Time Constraint:** Too busy for traditional courses or workshops

### Pain Points
1. **Can't tell what's real anymore** - Every vendor claims AI will 10x their business
2. **No vocabulary to challenge claims** - Lacks frameworks to ask sharp questions in pitches
3. **Second-guessing decisions** - Unsure if they're thinking clearly or just following hype
4. **Isolation** - Feels like everyone else "gets it" but they don't
5. **Analysis paralysis** - Too much information, not enough clarity

### Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal:** Make better AI decisions faster without relying on vendors
- **Motivations:**
  - Stay relevant as AI reshapes their industry
  - Protect company from expensive failed pilots
  - Build personal confidence in AI strategy conversations
  - Stop feeling behind or foolish in leadership meetings

### Behavioral Patterns
- Attends 2-3 AI webinars/month but gains no clarity
- Bookmarks articles about AI but rarely has time to read deeply
- Defers to technical team for AI decisions (despite being strategic leader)
- Hesitates to challenge vendor claims in sales meetings
- Actively seeks "no-BS" content that cuts through hype

### Success Criteria
After using Mindmaker, they can:
- ✅ Spot vendor theatre in the first 5 minutes of a pitch
- ✅ Ask sharp questions that vendors can't easily dodge
- ✅ Make AI decisions without second-guessing themselves
- ✅ Explain AI strategy clearly to board/investors
- ✅ Prioritize AI opportunities based on real value, not hype

## ICP #2: Executive Teams

### Demographics
- **Team Size:** 5-15 executives (C-suite + direct reports)
- **Company Size:** 100-2,000 employees
- **Industry:** Tech, SaaS, Finance, Healthcare, Professional Services
- **Revenue:** $25M-$300M ARR
- **Decision-Maker:** CEO, COO, Chief of Staff, Head of Strategy

### Team Characteristics
- **Fragmented thinking** - Everyone has different definitions of "AI strategy"
- **Misalignment** - No shared language for evaluating AI opportunities
- **Conflicting priorities** - Product wants to build, Sales wants to sell, Ops wants to automate
- **Expensive debates** - Weeks spent arguing about AI pilots without frameworks to decide

### Pain Points
1. **No shared mental models** - Team can't agree on what AI even means for the business
2. **Expensive misalignment** - Companies fund wrong pilots because execs aren't aligned
3. **Slow decision velocity** - Takes 3+ weeks to green-light AI initiatives due to confusion
4. **Vendor confusion** - Different execs get pitched different AI solutions, creating chaos
5. **No common language** - Can't challenge assumptions as a team because frameworks differ

### Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal:** Get entire leadership team thinking from the same AI operating system
- **Motivations:**
  - Stop wasting time in unproductive AI strategy debates
  - Align team before spending $500K+ on AI pilots
  - Build shared vocabulary to evaluate vendors as a unit
  - Make AI decisions 3x faster with confidence

### Behavioral Patterns
- Schedules multiple "AI strategy offsites" that don't resolve confusion
- Brings in consultants who deliver decks that die in email
- Experiences buyer's remorse on AI tools 3-6 months post-purchase
- Struggles to prioritize AI initiatives because no shared evaluation framework
- Defers AI decisions to "later" because alignment is too hard

### Success Criteria
After using Mindmaker, the team can:
- ✅ Operate from shared mental models when discussing AI
- ✅ Evaluate vendors using common frameworks (not personal opinions)
- ✅ Make AI decisions 3x faster as a cohesive unit
- ✅ Spot gaps in thinking before they become expensive mistakes
- ✅ Challenge each other's AI assumptions constructively

## ICP #3: Investment Firms (Partners & Advisors)

### Demographics
- **Role:** GP, Partner, Operating Partner, Platform Team, Investment Committee Member
- **Firm Type:** Venture Capital, Private Equity, Family Office, Advisory Firm
- **Portfolio Size:** 10-50 portfolio companies
- **Check Size:** $5M-$100M investments
- **Focus:** B2B SaaS, Enterprise Tech, Fintech, Healthcare Tech

### Firm Characteristics
- **Value-Add Focus** - Wants to provide strategic guidance beyond capital
- **Portfolio Risk** - Concerned about companies wasting money on bad AI bets
- **Operational Leverage** - Can't manually coach every portfolio CEO on AI
- **Competitive Pressure** - Other firms are marketing "AI expertise" as differentiator

### Pain Points
1. **Can't tell which portfolio companies will waste money on AI theatre**
2. **No scalable way to benchmark AI maturity across 10+ companies**
3. **Portfolio CEOs ask for AI advice, but partners lack frameworks to give it**
4. **Fear of backing companies that miss AI inflection points**
5. **Lack tools to spot cognitive gaps before they become ROI problems**

### Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal:** De-risk portfolio AI spend and add value beyond capital
- **Motivations:**
  - Protect portfolio companies from expensive failed pilots
  - Demonstrate operational expertise to LPs and founders
  - Spot cognitive gaps early (before they scale)
  - Build reputation as "AI-literate" investors

### Behavioral Patterns
- Sits in board meetings where AI gets discussed but lacks frameworks to contribute
- Sees pattern of portfolio companies buying same AI tools with different results
- Wants to help but doesn't want to become "AI consultant" to every CEO
- Seeks scalable playbooks that work across multiple portfolio companies
- Values frameworks that save time (not create more work)

### Success Criteria
After using Mindmaker, partners can:
- ✅ Benchmark AI literacy across entire portfolio in under 30 minutes
- ✅ Spot which CEOs will waste money on theatre vs substance
- ✅ Provide strategic AI guidance without needing to be technical experts
- ✅ Add measurable value beyond capital (demonstrable to LPs)
- ✅ Scale AI literacy infrastructure across portfolio without custom work

## Anti-Personas (Who We DON'T Serve)

### 1. Technical AI Engineers
- **Why:** They need technical training, not cognitive frameworks
- **Better Fit:** Technical courses, certifications, hands-on labs

### 2. Individual Contributors (Non-Leadership)
- **Why:** They need tool training, not strategic thinking frameworks
- **Better Fit:** Coursera, Udemy, company L&D programs

### 3. Startups <$5M Revenue
- **Why:** Too early-stage, typically can't afford strategic advisory
- **Better Fit:** Free resources, community content, DIY diagnostic

### 4. Enterprises Wanting Custom AI Strategy
- **Why:** We provide frameworks, not consulting deliverables
- **Better Fit:** McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte AI practices

### 5. "AI Believers" Looking for Validation
- **Why:** We're skeptical by design, not evangelical
- **Better Fit:** Vendor-led communities, AI influencer content
